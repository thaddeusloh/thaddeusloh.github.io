Urban Canopy: A Report on Trees Around Singapore

Observations on the island's streetside and park trees — from Angsana avenues to the rain trees of the expressways — and how a dense city keeps nearly half of itself under green cover.

1. A city in a garden

Singapore manages one of the densest urban tree populations in the world, with several million trees along roads, in parks, and across nature reserves. The greening effort dates to the late 1960s and has since matured into an islandwide canopy that shades roughly a quarter of the land area, moderating street-level temperatures and softening the skyline.

3. Reading the canopy

Heritage trees — individually registered giants such as the Tembusu on the five-dollar note — anchor the older landscapes, while newer plantings favour a wider species mix for resilience. On any walk, the canopy tells a layered story: pioneer coastal species near the shore, ornamental flowering avenues in town, and dipterocarp giants held in the central reserves.
