chapter xx · strange loops, or tangled hierarchies

You are back where you began

Twenty chambers, and the stair has climbed only to deliver you to its own first step. That is a strange loop: moving steadily upward through the levels of a system and arriving, transformed, at the place you started. Hofstadter's wager is that a self — an “I” — is exactly this kind of loop, a system that grew rich enough to point at itself.

The braid is closed. The cat, of course, already knew. MU.
